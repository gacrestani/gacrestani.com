# My story

My name is Giovanni Alberto Crestani. I am a Ph.D. student at Burke’s Lab, Oregon State University, where I am unraveling the misteries of Evolution using *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* as a model organism for Evolve and Resequence (E&R) experiments. My primary focus is on Computational Biology and Biological Data Science. I am also deeply passionate about Complexity Sciences, Systems Biology and Nonlinear Dynamics.

I received my undergrad degree in Biological Sciences (Licentiate) at Federal University of Santa Catarina, Brazil. There, I worked at molecular, microbiology, and immunology lab benches; at the computer, with genome annotation, microbial metagenomics, cancer RNA-Seq data, and building bioinformatics workflow; and at the field, collecting fuel-contaminated water and soil samples.

I was born and raised in Videira, Brazil. I had a fantastic childhood at my family’s farm, which taught me to respect and admire Nature. I got in touch with computers really early, which helped me develop a curiosity and passion that will last a lifetime.

My hobbies involve playing games and reading random blogs. I love going for hikes and swimming when the weather is nice! If it is not, I stay home playing all sorts of games and reading random blogs.
